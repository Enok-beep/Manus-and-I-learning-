## The Fractal Nature of Markets

### Key Insight: Ranges Within Ranges
- Every range contains **smaller ranges**.
- Every Wyckoff pattern contains **smaller Wyckoff patterns**.
- This is the **fractal nature** of the market.

### The Implication
- A **HTF Accumulation** will contain multiple **LTF Accumulations and Distributions**.
- A **HTF Distribution** will contain multiple **LTF Accumulations and Distributions**.
- Understanding this allows for **precise entries** within larger patterns.

## How to Use Wyckoff Within Wyckoff

### Step 1: Identify the HTF Pattern
- Determine if the HTF is in Accumulation or Distribution.
- Mark the key phases (A, B, C, D, E).

### Step 2: Zoom In to LTF
- Look for smaller Wyckoff patterns within the HTF phases.
- These LTF patterns provide **entry signals**.

### Step 3: Align LTF with HTF
- Only trade LTF patterns that **align** with the HTF direction.
- Example: In a HTF Accumulation, look for LTF Accumulations for long entries.

## The "Black and White" Rules

### Rule 1: HTF Determines Direction
- Never trade against the HTF pattern.
- LTF patterns are for **timing**, not **direction**.

### Rule 2: LTF Confirms Entry
- Wait for a LTF Wyckoff pattern to form at the HTF POI.
- This provides the **precise entry** with a tight stop loss.

### Rule 3: Alignment is Key
- The best trades occur when LTF and HTF patterns **align**.
- Example: LTF Spring within HTF Phase C = High probability long.
