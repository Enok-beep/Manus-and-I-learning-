## The History of Wyckoff

**Richard D. Wyckoff** (1873-1934) was a legendary stock market trader and educator who developed a methodology for understanding market behavior based on the actions of "Smart Money" (institutional traders).

### Key Insight:
> "As you can probably see in the title, I clearly..."

Wyckoff's methodology is based on the observation that:
1. Markets are manipulated by large institutional players.
2. These players leave "footprints" in the price action.
3. By understanding these footprints, retail traders can trade *with* the institutions.

## The Three Laws of Wyckoff

### Law 1: The Law of Supply and Demand
- Price moves up when demand exceeds supply.
- Price moves down when supply exceeds demand.
- This is the foundation of all price movement.

### Law 2: The Law of Cause and Effect
- Every move in the market has a "Cause" (accumulation or distribution).
- The "Effect" (trend) is proportional to the "Cause."
- Larger accumulation = larger uptrend. Larger distribution = larger downtrend.

### Law 3: The Law of Effort vs. Result
- Volume is the "Effort" and price movement is the "Result."
- If effort (volume) is high but result (price movement) is low, the move is likely to fail.
- This is the basis for identifying "Stopping Volume" and "No Demand."

## The Wyckoff Market Cycle

The market moves in a predictable cycle:
1. **Accumulation**: Smart Money buys from weak hands.
2. **Markup**: Price trends upward.
3. **Distribution**: Smart Money sells to weak hands.
4. **Markdown**: Price trends downward.

> **Wyckoff is the "Why" behind everything we've learned.**
> - Market Structure is the *result* of Wyckoff cycles.
> - Ranges are the *location* of accumulation and distribution.

By understanding Wyckoff, you understand the *intent* of Smart Money, not just the *footprints* they leave behind.

## Key Terminology (Preview)

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PS (Preliminary Support)** | First sign of buying interest after a downtrend. |
| **SC (Selling Climax)** | Panic selling that marks the low of the range. |
| **AR (Automatic Rally)** | The first bounce after the SC. |
| **ST (Secondary Test)** | A test of the SC low to confirm demand. |
| **Spring** | A false breakdown below the range to grab liquidity. |
| **SOS (Sign of Strength)** | A strong move that breaks the range high. |
| **LPS (Last Point of Support)** | The final pullback before the markup phase. |
