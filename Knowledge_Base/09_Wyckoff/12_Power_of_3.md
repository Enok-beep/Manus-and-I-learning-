## The Power of 3 Framework

The Power of 3 is a **three-phase market cycle** that repeats at every timeframe:

| Phase | Name | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Accumulation/Distribution | The range where positions are built |
| **2** | Manipulation | The false breakout (liquidity grab) |
| **3** | Distribution/Expansion | The real move in the intended direction |

## Power of 3 in Bullish Context (AMD)

### Phase 1: Accumulation
- Price consolidates in a range.
- Smart Money is **buying** from retail sellers.

### Phase 2: Manipulation
- Price breaks **below** the range (Spring).
- Retail traders go short (stop losses triggered).
- Smart Money **buys more** at discounted prices.

### Phase 3: Distribution (Expansion)
- Price breaks **above** the range.
- Smart Money **sells** to retail buyers.
- The markup phase begins.

## Power of 3 in Bearish Context (AMD)

### Phase 1: Distribution
- Price consolidates in a range.
- Smart Money is **selling** to retail buyers.

### Phase 2: Manipulation
- Price breaks **above** the range (UTAD).
- Retail traders go long (stop losses triggered).
- Smart Money **sells more** at premium prices.

### Phase 3: Distribution (Expansion)
- Price breaks **below** the range.
- Smart Money **covers** from retail sellers.
- The markdown phase begins.

## Power of 3 and Wyckoff

## The "Black and White" Rules for Power of 3

### Identification:
1. **Range**: Identify a clear consolidation range.
2. **Manipulation**: Wait for a false breakout (sweep of range high or low).
3. **Confirmation**: Wait for price to re-enter the range and break the opposite side.

### Entry Points:
1. **Manipulation Entry**: Enter at the false breakout (requires LTF confirmation).
2. **Confirmation Entry**: Enter after price re-enters the range and shows intent.
3. **BOS Entry**: Enter after the break of the opposite side of the range.

### Stop Loss Placement:
- Beyond the manipulation extreme (Spring low or UTAD high).

> - It distills the complex Wyckoff schematics into three simple phases.
> - It applies to **every timeframe** and **every market**.
> - It is the **foundation** of the "Smart Money" approach.
