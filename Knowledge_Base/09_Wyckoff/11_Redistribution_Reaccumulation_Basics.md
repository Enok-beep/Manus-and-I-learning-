## Reaccumulation vs. Accumulation

| Feature | Accumulation | Reaccumulation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Context** | Downtrend (Reversal) | Uptrend (Continuation) |
| **Purpose** | Start a new uptrend | Continue an existing uptrend |
| **Location** | At market lows | In the middle of an uptrend |
| **Result** | Markup begins | Markup continues |

## Redistribution vs. Distribution

| Feature | Distribution | Redistribution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Context** | Uptrend (Reversal) | Downtrend (Continuation) |
| **Purpose** | Start a new downtrend | Continue an existing downtrend |
| **Location** | At market highs | In the middle of a downtrend |
| **Result** | Markdown begins | Markdown continues |

## How to Identify Reaccumulation

### Key Characteristics:
1. **Uptrend Context**: Price is already in an established uptrend.
2. **Pullback Range**: Price consolidates, creating a range.
3. **Spring/Test**: Price may sweep the range low (liquidity grab).
4. **Continuation**: Price breaks out to the upside and continues the uptrend.

### The "Black and White" Rule:
> **If the range forms in an uptrend and breaks up, it's Reaccumulation.**
> **If the range forms in an uptrend and breaks down, it was Distribution.**

## How to Identify Redistribution

### Key Characteristics:
1. **Downtrend Context**: Price is already in an established downtrend.
2. **Rally Range**: Price consolidates, creating a range.
3. **UTAD/Test**: Price may sweep the range high (liquidity grab).
4. **Continuation**: Price breaks out to the downside and continues the downtrend.

### The "Black and White" Rule:
> **If the range forms in a downtrend and breaks down, it's Redistribution.**
> **If the range forms in a downtrend and breaks up, it was Accumulation.**

## Trading Reaccumulation

### Entry Points:
1. **Spring Entry**: Enter long at the spring (sweep of range low).
2. **LPS Entry**: Enter long on the pullback after the SOS.
3. **BOS Retest Entry**: Enter long after the BOS retests the range high as support.

### Stop Loss Placement:
- Below the Spring low.
- Below the LPS low.
- Below the range high (for BOS retest entry).

## Trading Redistribution

### Entry Points:
1. **UTAD Entry**: Enter short at the UTAD (sweep of range high).
2. **LPSY Entry**: Enter short on the pullback after the SOW.
3. **BOS Retest Entry**: Enter short after the BOS retests the range low as resistance.

### Stop Loss Placement:
- Above the UTAD high.
- Above the LPSY high.
- Above the range low (for BOS retest entry).

## The "Grand Secret" of Continuation Patterns

> **Reaccumulation and Redistribution are the "Staircasing" of Wyckoff.**
> - They are the **building blocks** of a sustained trend.
> - Every major trend is made up of multiple Re-patterns.
> - Understanding this allows you to **add to winning positions** safely.

## The "Ultimate Interconnection"

> **Continuation patterns connect Wyckoff to Market Structure.**
> - A **Higher Low** in an uptrend is often a **Reaccumulation**.
> - A **Lower High** in a downtrend is often a **Redistribution**.
> - This is the **solution** to the complexity of "is this a reversal or a pullback."
