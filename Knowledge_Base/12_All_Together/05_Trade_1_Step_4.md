## The Scenario Planning Process

### Scenario 1: Accumulation Model
From the transcript:
> "this could very well be an accumulation model into an area of demand on E"

This scenario involves:
1. Price consolidates in a range (Wyckoff accumulation).
2. Price sweeps the low (Spring).
3. Price reverses and breaks out to the upside.

### Scenario 2: Continuation to Resistance
From the transcript:
> "we know there's nothing but inefficiency above. So the price could very well shoot right back up through it."

This scenario involves:
1. Price breaks out of the range.
2. Price fills the inefficiency (FVG) above.
3. Price reaches the next logical resistance.

### Scenario 3: Rejection and Continuation Down
From the transcript:
> "If it continues that downwards trend, that would make sense to reject from an area like this, right?"

This scenario involves:
1. Price fails to break out.
2. Price rejects from a supply zone.
3. Price continues the downward trend.

## The Execution Decision Tree

### Before Entering:
1. **Identify the most likely scenario** based on the HTF bias.
2. **Mark the key levels** for each scenario.
3. **Define the invalidation point** for each scenario.

### During Execution:
1. **Wait for the LTF confirmation** (from Step 3).
2. **Enter when the scenario plays out** as expected.
3. **Skip the trade if the scenario is unclear**.

## The "Inefficiency" Concept

### From the Transcript:
> "we know there's nothing but inefficiency above."

This refers to the **Fair Value Gap (FVG)** from Chapter 4:
- Inefficiency = price moved too fast, leaving a gap.
- The market tends to "fill" these gaps.
- This creates a "magnet" for price.

### How to Use Inefficiency:
1. **Identify the FVG** on the chart.
2. **Expect price to fill it** at some point.
3. **Use it as a target** or a reason to hold the trade.

## The "Logical Area" Concept

### From the Transcript:
> "And we know the next logical area for resistance is right here."

This refers to the **Protected/Targeted** levels from Chapter 3:
- A "logical area" is a structural point that has a "job" to do.
- Resistance = a level that should stop price from going higher.
- Support = a level that should stop price from going lower.

### How to Use Logical Areas:
1. **Identify the next structural target**.
2. **Use it as a take-profit level**.
3. **Adjust your position size** based on the distance.
