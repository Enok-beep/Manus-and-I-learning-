## The Three Entry Methods

### Method 1: High Risk Entry
- **Description**: Entering at the POI without LTF confirmation.
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Reward Potential**: HIGHEST
- **When to Use**: When you have extreme confidence in the HTF setup.

### Method 2: Medium Risk Entry
- **Description**: Entering after initial LTF reaction at the POI.
- **Risk Level**: MEDIUM
- **Reward Potential**: MEDIUM-HIGH
- **When to Use**: When you want a balance between risk and reward.

### Method 3: Low Risk Entry
- **Description**: Entering after full LTF BOS confirmation.
- **Risk Level**: LOW
- **Reward Potential**: LOWER (due to later entry)
- **When to Use**: When you want maximum confirmation.

## The Risk/Reward Trade-Off

| Entry Method | Risk | Reward | Stop Distance | Win Rate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| High Risk | HIGH | HIGHEST | Tight | Lower |
| Medium Risk | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Medium | Medium |
| Low Risk | LOW | LOWER | Wider | Higher |

### The Key Insight:

- Your risk tolerance.
- Your confidence in the setup.
- Your trading style.

- This is the **Low Risk** method.
- Wait for the LTF to confirm the reversal.
- Enter on the BOS close (body close, not wick).

## Connection to Previous Lessons

- **Body Close** is required for a valid BOS.
- **Wick Breaks** are not confirmation.

### From Supply and Demand (Chapter 4):
- **POI** is where you look for entries.
- **Zone Reaction** is the first sign of interest.
- **Zone Confirmation** is the full reversal signal.

### From Orderflow (Chapter 5):
- **RIMC Cycle** determines the entry timing.
- **Mitigation** is the touch of the POI.
- **Continuation** is the move away from the POI.

## The Decision Framework

### Before Entering:
1. **Assess your confidence** in the HTF setup.
2. **Choose your entry method** based on confidence.
3. **Calculate your position size** based on stop distance.
4. **Execute the trade** when the conditions are met.

### The Professional Approach:
> **There is no "best" entry method.**
> - High risk entries can be profitable with high confidence setups.
> - Low risk entries can be profitable with consistent execution.
> - The key is to match your entry method to your trading style.
