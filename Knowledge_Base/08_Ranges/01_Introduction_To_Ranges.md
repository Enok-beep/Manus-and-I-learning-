# Lesson 01: Introduction To Ranges

This lesson (11:50) introduces the concept of **Ranges** and explains how they fit into the overall trading framework.

## What is a Range?

> "So what is a range? So a range is an area of consolidation prior to a break in market structure for either a continuation or a reversal."

### Key Definition:
- A **Range** is an area of **consolidation**.
- It occurs **before** a break in market structure.
- The break can lead to either a **continuation** or a **reversal**.

## Range Identification Example

> "So essentially what we're looking at here is an example where I have this higher timeframe market structure drawing and green where it's an uptrend, right? Very clear as day uptrend, nothing fancy where we can pretend this is four hour market structure. We have a four hour higher high, we have a four hour higher low and near the higher high and near that higher low you can see..."

### Key Insight:
- Ranges form **within** the larger market structure.
- In an uptrend, ranges form between the **Higher High** and **Higher Low**.
- The range is where price "rests" before the next structural move.

## The "Four Hour" Example

> "...we have a four hour higher high, we have a four hour higher low and near the higher high and near that higher low you can see..."

### Key Insight:
- The **4H timeframe** is often used to identify significant ranges.
- The range boundaries are defined by the **structural points** (HH/HL or LH/LL).

## The "Grand Secret" of Ranges

The "grand secret" is that **Ranges are the "Pause" before the "Move."**
- The market doesn't move in straight lines; it moves in **waves**.
- Each wave has a **pause** (the range) and a **move** (the BOS).
- By identifying the range, you can anticipate the next move.

## Practical Application

### Identifying a Range:
1. Identify the **HTF structure** (bullish or bearish).
2. Find the **most recent structural points** (HH/HL or LH/LL).
3. The area between these points is the **range**.
4. Wait for price to **consolidate** within the range.
5. Look for a **BOS** to signal the end of the range.

### The "Range Boundaries" Rule:
- The **top** of the range is the most recent **swing high**.
- The **bottom** of the range is the most recent **swing low**.
- These boundaries are where **liquidity** accumulates.

## The "Black and White" Rule

> **A range is defined by the most recent swing high and swing low on your chosen timeframe.**

- If price is between these two points, you are **in a range**.
- If price breaks one of these points, the range is **over**.
