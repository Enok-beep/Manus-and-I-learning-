## The "Grand Secret" of Entry

The "grand secret" is **Confluence of Confirmation**. 
- You don't enter a trade based on one signal.
- You enter when **multiple levels of structure** align.
- This is the "hand in hand" relationship Jayson mentions.

## The Entry Checklist

### Step-by-Step Process:
1. **HTF Bias**: Determine the overall direction (bullish or bearish).
2. **Swing Structure**: Identify the swing highs and lows.
3. **Internal Structure**: Wait for internal structure to align with the swing bias.
4. **POI Identification**: Find the S&D zone where you expect price to react.
5. **LTF Confirmation**: Drop to the LTF and wait for a CHoCH or BOS in your direction.
6. **Execute**: Enter the trade with your stop behind the relevant structural point.

## Connection to Previous Chapters

| Concept | Connection to Entry |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Risk Management** | The entry point determines your stop loss distance and position size. |
| **Market Structure** | The entry is based on structural alignment across multiple timeframes. |
| **Supply and Demand** | The entry is at a validated S&D zone. |
| **Orderflow** | The entry is confirmed by a CHoCH or BOS on the LTF (the "Initiation" phase). |

## The "Higher Probability" Factor

The key to "higher probability" is **waiting for all the pieces to align**. 
- If the HTF is bullish but the internal structure is bearish, **wait**.
- If the internal structure is bullish but you're not at a POI, **wait**.
- If you're at a POI but there's no LTF confirmation, **wait**.

Patience is the ultimate edge.
