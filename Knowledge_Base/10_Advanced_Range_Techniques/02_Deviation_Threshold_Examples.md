## Core Concept: Deviation Thresholds

A **Deviation Threshold** is the specific level where a deviation is considered "valid" or "significant." Not every break of a range boundary is a tradable deviation.

## The Threshold Framework

### Key Thresholds:
1. **Minor Deviation**: 0% to -27% (often just noise).
2. **Standard Deviation**: -27% to -62% (most common).
3. **Deep Deviation**: -62% to -100% (high probability reversal).
4. **Extreme Deviation**: Beyond -100% (rare, but powerful).

## The "Black and White" Rules for Deviation Thresholds

### Rule 1: Minimum Threshold
> **A valid deviation must reach at least the -27% level.**
> - Anything less is likely just "noise."
> - Wait for price to reach a significant threshold.

### Rule 2: Rejection Confirmation
> **A valid deviation must show rejection at the threshold.**
> - Look for wicks, reversal candles, or LTF CHoCH.
> - No rejection = no trade.

### Rule 3: Context Matters
> **The significance of a deviation depends on the HTF context.**
> - A deviation at a HTF S&D zone is more powerful.
> - A deviation with HTF liquidity sweep is more powerful.

## Practical Application

### Step-by-Step Process:
1. **Identify the Range**: Use HTF to find clear consolidation.
2. **Apply Fibonacci**: From range high to low (or vice versa).
3. **Wait for Deviation**: Price must break the boundary.
4. **Check Threshold**: Did it reach -27%, -62%, or deeper?
5. **Confirm Rejection**: Look for reversal signals.
6. **Enter Trade**: On LTF confirmation (CHoCH or BOS).

### Entry Refinement:
| Threshold | Entry Aggressiveness | Stop Loss |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-27%** | Conservative | Beyond -62% |
| **-62%** | Standard | Beyond -100% |
| **-100%** | Aggressive | Beyond deviation extreme |

## Deviation Thresholds and Confluence

### High-Probability Setups:
1. **Deviation + HTF S&D Zone**: The deviation sweeps into a HTF supply or demand zone.
2. **Deviation + Liquidity Sweep**: The deviation sweeps a clear liquidity pool.
3. **Deviation + Wyckoff Event**: The deviation aligns with a Spring or UTAD.

## The "Grand Secret" of Deviation Thresholds

> **Not all deviations are created equal.**
> - The **depth** of the deviation determines its **probability**.
> - The **context** of the deviation determines its **magnitude**.
> - The **confirmation** of the deviation determines its **entry**.
