# Lesson 03: Advanced Power of 3

This lesson (17:06) builds on the **Power of 3** concept from the Wyckoff chapter and applies it specifically to **Range Trading**.

## Core Concept: Advanced Power of 3

The **Power of 3** is a framework that describes how price moves in three phases:
1. **Accumulation/Distribution** (The Setup)
2. **Manipulation** (The Trap)
3. **Distribution/Accumulation** (The Move)

In the context of **Advanced Range Trading**, this becomes:
1. **Range Formation** (The Setup)
2. **Deviation** (The Trap)
3. **Expansion** (The Move)

### Key Insight:
The **Confined Zone** is the range boundary that, once established, becomes the "line in the sand." If price deviates beyond this zone and then returns, it signals the **Manipulation Phase** is complete.

## Advanced Power of 3 Framework

### Phase 1: Range Formation (Accumulation/Distribution)
- Price consolidates within a defined range.
- The range high and low become the **Confined Zone**.
- This is where Smart Money is building positions.

### Phase 2: Deviation (Manipulation)
- Price breaks the range boundary.
- This is the **"Trap"** that induces retail traders to enter.
- The deviation sweeps liquidity and triggers stop losses.

### Phase 3: Expansion (Distribution/Accumulation)
- Price reverses back into the range.
- Price then expands to the opposite side of the range.
- This is where Smart Money takes profit.

## The "Black and White" Rules

### Rule 1: Define the Confined Zone
> **The range must be clearly defined before looking for a Power of 3 setup.**
> - Use the HTF to identify the range.
> - Mark the range high and low.

### Rule 2: Wait for Deviation
> **A valid Power of 3 setup requires a deviation beyond the Confined Zone.**
> - The deviation must reach a significant threshold (-27% or deeper).
> - The deviation must show rejection.

### Rule 3: Confirm the Reversal
> **The reversal must be confirmed on the LTF before entering.**
> - Look for a CHoCH or BOS on the LTF.
> - The confirmation must occur within the Confined Zone.

### Rule 4: Target the Opposite Side
> **The target is always the opposite side of the range.**
> - If the deviation was below the range, target the range high.
> - If the deviation was above the range, target the range low.

## Practical Application

### Step-by-Step Process:
1. **Identify the Range**: Use HTF to find clear consolidation.
2. **Mark the Confined Zone**: Draw the range high and low.
3. **Wait for Deviation**: Price must break the boundary.
4. **Confirm Rejection**: Look for reversal signals at the deviation.
5. **Enter on LTF Confirmation**: CHoCH or BOS back into the range.
6. **Target the Opposite Side**: Set TP at the opposite range boundary.

### Entry Refinement:
| Phase | Action | Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Range Formation** | Wait | No trade |
| **Deviation** | Prepare | No trade |
| **Rejection** | Enter | Stop beyond deviation |
| **Expansion** | Manage | Trail stop |
