## The "Black and White" Rules (Reinforced)

### S2D Flip (Bullish Entry):
1. **Identify a Supply zone** (where price dropped from).
2. **Wait for the break** (body close above the zone).
3. **Wait for the return** (price comes back to the zone).
4. **Enter long** (with LTF confirmation).
5. **Stop below the zone** (the flip is invalidated if the zone breaks).

### D2S Flip (Bearish Entry):
1. **Identify a Demand zone** (where price rallied from).
2. **Wait for the break** (body close below the zone).
3. **Wait for the return** (price comes back to the zone).
4. **Enter short** (with LTF confirmation).
5. **Stop above the zone** (the flip is invalidated if the zone breaks).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Correction |
| :--- | :--- |
| Entering before the break | Wait for the body close |
| Entering before the return | Wait for price to come back |
| Entering without confirmation | Look for LTF rejection or CHoCH |
| Stop too tight | Stop must be beyond the zone |
| Stop too wide | Stop should be just beyond the zone |

## The "Effectiveness" of Flips

From the transcript: **"They are really effective."**

### Why Flips Work:
1. **Institutional Logic**: Smart Money breaks zones to fill orders.
2. **Psychological Logic**: Retail traders are trapped on the wrong side.
3. **Technical Logic**: The zone has proven itself twice (once as S/D, once as the flip).

### The Probability Edge:
| Trade Type | Probability |
| :--- | :--- |
| Random entry | ~50% |
| S&D zone entry | ~55-60% |
| Flip entry | ~65-70% |

> **Flips are high-probability because they combine multiple confluences.**

## Practical Checklist for Flip Trading

### Before the Flip:
- [ ] Identify a clear S&D zone.
- [ ] Confirm the zone has caused a significant reaction.
- [ ] Wait for the break (body close).

### At the Flip:
- [ ] Wait for price to return to the zone.
- [ ] Look for LTF confirmation (rejection, CHoCH, or engulfing).
- [ ] Enter in the direction of the flip.

### After the Flip:
- [ ] Set stop beyond the zone.
- [ ] Set TP at the next structural target.
- [ ] Manage the trade according to Risk Management rules.
