## Example 1: Bearish Deviation Setup

### The Setup:
1. **Range Identified**: Price was consolidating in a clear range.
2. **Deviation Above**: Price broke above the range high.
3. **Aggressive Pull Away**: Price then broke down from the range with strong momentum.

### Key Observation:
> **An "aggressive pull away" from a range signals the start of a new trend.**
> - This is the **Initiation** phase of the RIMC cycle (from Orderflow).
> - The aggressive move creates an **FVG** (Fair Value Gap) that will need to be filled.

## The "Aggressive Pull Away" Concept

### What is an Aggressive Pull Away?
An **Aggressive Pull Away** is a strong, impulsive move away from a range boundary. It indicates:
1. **Smart Money has entered** the market.
2. **Retail traders are trapped** on the wrong side.
3. **A new trend is beginning**.

### What Happens After an Aggressive Pull Away?
According to the RIMC cycle:
1. **Range**: The consolidation before the move.
2. **Initiation**: The aggressive pull away.
3. **Mitigation**: Price returns to mitigate the FVG or S&D zone.
4. **Continuation**: Price continues in the direction of the initiation.

## Practical Application from the Example

### Step-by-Step Analysis:
1. **Identify the Range**: Mark the high and low of the consolidation.
2. **Observe the Deviation**: Price breaks above the range high.
3. **Wait for the Aggressive Pull Away**: Price breaks down with strong momentum.
4. **Identify the FVG**: The aggressive move creates an imbalance.
5. **Wait for Mitigation**: Price returns to fill the FVG.
6. **Enter on Confirmation**: LTF CHoCH or BOS in the direction of the initiation.

### Entry Refinement:
| Phase | Action | Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Deviation** | Observe | No trade |
| **Aggressive Pull Away** | Prepare | No trade |
| **Mitigation** | Enter | Stop beyond the deviation |
