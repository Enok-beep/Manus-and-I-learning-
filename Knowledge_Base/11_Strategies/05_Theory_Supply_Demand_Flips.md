## What is a Supply/Demand Flip?

A **Supply/Demand Flip** occurs when a zone that was previously acting as **Supply** (resistance) becomes **Demand** (support), or vice versa.

### Supply to Demand (S2D) Flip:
- A **Supply zone** (where price previously dropped from) is **broken**.
- Price then **returns** to that zone.
- The zone now acts as **Demand** (support).

### Demand to Supply (D2S) Flip:
- A **Demand zone** (where price previously rallied from) is **broken**.
- Price then **returns** to that zone.
- The zone now acts as **Supply** (resistance).

## Why S&D Flips are Powerful

| Standard Transition | S&D Flip |
| :--- | :--- |
| Uses market structure alone | Combines market structure with S&D zones |
| Entry at any pullback | Entry at a **specific, validated zone** |
| Stop loss based on structure | Stop loss based on **zone invalidation** |
| Good probability | **Higher probability** |

### The Key Insight:
> **S&D Flips provide a "second layer" of validation.**
> - The zone has already proven itself by causing a reaction.
> - The break of the zone confirms a shift in market sentiment.
> - The return to the zone offers a high-probability entry.

## The "Black and White" Rules

### Rule 1: Identify the Original Zone
> **The zone must have caused a significant reaction.**
> - For S2D: A Supply zone that caused a drop.
> - For D2S: A Demand zone that caused a rally.

### Rule 2: Wait for the Break
> **The zone must be broken with a Body Close.**
> - This confirms the shift in market sentiment.
> - The break should be impulsive, not a slow grind.

### Rule 3: Wait for the Return
> **Price must return to the broken zone.**
> - This is the "flip" in action.
> - The return should be a pullback, not a reversal.

### Rule 4: Look for Confirmation
> **Confirmation can be a candle pattern or LTF structure shift.**
> - A rejection candle at the zone.
> - A CHoCH or BOS on the LTF.

### Rule 5: Enter and Manage
> **Enter on confirmation with stop beyond the zone.**
> - Stop Loss: Beyond the zone that flipped.
> - Take Profit: Based on market structure targets.

## S&D Flips vs. Standard Transitions

### Standard Transition:
1. Wait for BOS/CHoCH.
2. Enter on any pullback.
3. Stop loss below the structural point.

### S&D Flip:
1. Wait for BOS/CHoCH **that breaks a zone**.
2. Enter **only when price returns to the broken zone**.
3. Stop loss beyond the **zone** (often tighter than structural stop).

### Which is Better?
> **S&D Flips are better when you can identify them.**
> - They provide a more precise entry.
> - They often have tighter stop losses.
> - They have a higher probability of success.

> **Standard Transitions are easier to find.**
> - They don't require zone identification.
> - They are more frequent.
> - They are a good fallback when no flip is available.

## The "Grand Secret" of S&D Flips

> **S&D Flips are the "Smart Money" entry model.**
> - Smart Money accumulates at Demand zones.
> - When they are ready to move price, they **break** the opposing Supply zone.
> - They then **return** to the broken zone to fill any remaining orders.
> - This is your opportunity to enter **with** Smart Money.

### Why This Works:
1. **The zone has proven itself** by causing a reaction.
2. **The break confirms intent** to move in the new direction.
3. **The return is the "last chance"** to get on board.
4. **Smart Money is defending the zone** on the return.
