# Lesson 01: Liquidity Introduction

This lesson (14:00) introduces the concept of **Liquidity** and explains why it is the "fuel" that drives all market movements.

## What is Liquidity?

> "All right guys, we're gonna talk about what is liquidity. So liquidity refers to how easy and how quickly an asset such as a stock or a cryptocurrency like Bitcoin can be bought or sold in the market without significantly impacting or affecting its price."

### Definition:
- **Liquidity** is a measure of how easily an asset can be converted into cash or stable coins without significantly impacting the price.

> "So in simple terms, it's basically a measure of how liquid or fluid an asset is, meaning how readily it can be converted into cash or stable coins if looking at cryptocurrency without majorly impacting the price. So the price doesn't fluctuate..."

### Key Insight:
- **High Liquidity** = Easy to buy/sell without moving the price.
- **Low Liquidity** = Difficult to buy/sell without moving the price.

## Why Liquidity Matters for Trading

### The "Grand Secret" of Liquidity:
- **Liquidity is the "Fuel" for price movement.**
- Price doesn't move randomly; it moves **from one pool of liquidity to the next**.
- Understanding liquidity allows you to **predict where price will go**.

## Types of Liquidity

### 1. Resting Liquidity:
- Orders that are **waiting** to be filled (stop losses, limit orders).
- These are the "targets" that price moves towards.

### 2. Active Liquidity:
- Orders that are **actively** being executed.
- These are the "fuel" that moves price.

## The Liquidity Hierarchy

The video shows a diagram that illustrates the hierarchy:
1. **Market Structure** (the foundation)
2. **Supply & Demand** (the zones)
3. **Liquidity** (the fuel)
4. **Execution** (the entry)

### Key Insight:
- You cannot understand **Execution** without understanding **Liquidity**.
- Liquidity is the "missing link" between analysis and action.

## Practical Application

### The "Liquidity Hunt":
- Smart Money **hunts** liquidity by pushing price to areas where stop losses are clustered.
- Once the liquidity is consumed, price reverses.
- This is why "stop hunts" happen so frequently.
